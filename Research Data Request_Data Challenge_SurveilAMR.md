Summary of Research:
Background and Summary of Proposed Research (SurveilAMR)

Antimicrobial resistance (AMR) is among the greatest threats to modern medicine: infections that were once easily treatable now fail more often, prolong illness, raise costs, and increase mortality. Across health systems especially in low- and middle-income settings resistance data is increasingly collected, yet it remains fragmented, analyzed slowly, and rarely translated into guidance that clinicians and public health teams can use at the moment decisions are made.

SurveilAMR addresses this gap by applying artificial intelligence to structured surveillance data from the Vivli AMR Register. The project integrates organism, susceptibility, and contextual metadata to detect resistance trends, compare patterns across sites and regions, and generate timely, interpretable dashboards and alerts for clinical and population-level decision-making transforming a growing global data asset into actionable intelligence for frontline use.

Improving patient outcomes. By surfacing local and regional resistance profiles earlier, SurveilAMR supports more appropriate empiric antibiotic choices particularly where culture results are delayed reducing treatment failure, complications, and length of hospital stay.

Strengthening stewardship. Decision-support outputs will help clinicians and antimicrobial stewardship programs identify high-risk resistance patterns, prioritize targeted therapy, and reduce unnecessary broad-spectrum antibiotic use helping preserve antibiotic effectiveness.

Informing public health practice. Automated trend detection, geographic mapping, and early-warning signals will enable faster updates to treatment guidelines, more targeted interventions, and earlier response to emerging resistance threats.

Strengthening health systems. SurveilAMR embeds usable analytics into routine surveillance workflows, improving data interpretation, reporting efficiency, and the capacity of health systems to monitor and respond to AMR at scale.

Together, these aims advance the 2026 Vivli AMR Surveillance Data Challenge by expanding the use of the AMR Register, enabling new analytical tools, and ensuring surveillance data directly improves clinical care, prescribing practices, and public health action.
